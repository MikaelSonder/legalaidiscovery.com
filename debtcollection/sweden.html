DEBT COLLECTION

KINGDOM OF SWEDEN
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Debt collection in Sweden is a regulated recovery function covering overdue claims, debtor communication, lawful notice, and escalation where payment is not achieved voluntarily. The Swedish model separates amicable recovery from formal enforcement and requires documentation, proportionality, privacy awareness, and respect for debtor rights throughout the process.
OBJECT DEFINITION
DEFINITIONThe regulated professional function responsible for pursuing payment of overdue claims, managing debtor communications, applying lawful collection measures, and supporting recovery through compliant pre-enforcement and enforcement processes under Swedish debt collection law, data protection rules and applicable procedural law.

PROCESS FLOW
1. TRIGGERAn unpaid invoice, overdue claim, or disputed receivable enters the collection workflow.
2. VALIDATIONThe claim is checked for contract basis, due date, supporting records, and collection eligibility.
3. NOTICEA reminder or collection demand is issued in accordance with lawful notice requirements.
4. CONTACTDebtor communication is carried out to clarify the debt, confirm status, and encourage voluntary payment.
5. ARRANGEMENTIf appropriate, a payment plan or settlement path is assessed and documented.
6. ESCALATIONUnresolved or contested matters are reviewed for further collection steps or legal referral.
7. CLOSEThe case is closed, settled, transferred, or referred onward depending on outcome.

SWEDISH COLLECTION MODEL
SWEDISH MODELDebt collection in Sweden is a regulated process that separates amicable recovery, licensed collection activities and formal enforcement. It requires lawful notice, proper documentation, prudent conduct and respect for debtor rights and privacy.
INTERNATIONAL POSITIONProvides a structured recovery framework suitable for domestic claims, cross-border receivables and outsourced collections where Swedish legal standards must be maintained.
PROFESSIONAL EXPECTATIONLawfulness • Proportionality • Documentation • Traceability • Dispute Sensitivity • Data Minimisation • Professional Conduct
TYPICAL QUESTIONS
WHAT IS IT?A regulated recovery function for overdue claims in Sweden.
WHEN DOES IT APPLY?When an invoice, claim, or receivable remains unpaid after the normal due date.
WHAT HAPPENS FIRST?The claim is validated and a lawful notice or reminder is issued.
CAN IT BE DISPUTED?Yes. Disputed matters require review before escalation.
WHAT IF PAYMENT DOES NOT ARRIVE?The matter may move to further collection steps or legal referral.
WHY IS DOCUMENTATION IMPORTANT?Because collection must remain traceable, proportionate, and legally defensible.
